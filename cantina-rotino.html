Cantina Rotino Spaghetti No. 3

Patriotic Packaging

Spaghetti No. 3 is the first product of Cantina Rotino, a new premium Italian-sourced food brand for the Australian market. This product won a Silver Award for Visual Communication in the 2017 Design Institute of Australia SA/NT Awards, was recognised with an Outstanding Achievement Award by How Magazine and was a finalist for Distintion in Packaging in the national 2017 Australian Graphic Design Association Awards.

Working as part of a team my role in this project included talking with the client to identify the brands target audience, creating the overall Cantina Rotino branding and designing the packaging. Housed in a cardboard tube, the aim of this spaghetti packaging was to create something beautiful and practical that the buyer would not want to throw away.

The product branding is contemporary, bold and passionately Italian, taking a minimalist modernist aesthetic and influence of Futurism (despite Futurists supposed disdain for pasta). The logo takes its form from the shape of a pillar in a traditional Italian cantina (cellar). Aimed at a young, urban professional audience who like to entertain, the product stands out on the mostly dull and conservative supermarket pasta shelf.
